    Canada labor code part two

    Application

    This will detail the application of the labor code and define some of the common terms that will be used throughout. It is best to use them in context to assist with understanding, explaining them if needed as the employees needs are determined as per the labour code.

    Canada Labour Code 122.3 (1)

    122.3 (1) An employee with a special need shall be given any direction, notice, information, instruction or training that is required to be given to employees under this Part by any method of communication that readily permits the employee to receive it, including braille, large print, audio tape, computer disk, sign language and verbal communication.

    The purpose of the code

    The purpose of Part II of the Canada Labour Code is to prevent work place related accidents, injury and occupational diseases. It is not to punish workers but to keep them safe and allow them to work with their employer to create a safe work environment.

    Prescribe

    prescribe means prescribe by regulation of the Governor in Council or determine in accordance with rules prescribed by regulation of the Governor in Council;

    "prescribe" means to establish or set out rules, regulations, or standards that must be followed. When something is prescribed, it is required or mandated by law. For example, the Canada Labour Code may prescribe certain safety standards that employers must follow in order to ensure the health and safety of their employees. Essentially, "prescribe" is a way of saying that something is mandatory or required by law.

    Application to other persons

    CLC Section 167 subsection 1.1

    Subject to subsection (1.2), this Part applies to any person who is not an employee but who performs for an employer to which this Part applies activities whose primary purpose is to enable the person to acquire knowledge or experience, and to the employer, as if that person were an employee of the employer, and every provision of this Part must be read accordingly.

    Simply put, if someone is doing work for a company, but they are not officially an employee, and the work they are doing is meant to help them learn or gain experience, then the rules in Part II of the Canada Labour Code still apply to them. This means that the company has to treat them like an employee and follow all the same rules that they would for a regular employee.

    Definitions

    These will be defined here but are best used in context when the relevent section is reached.

    Danger

    In simple terms, danger is defined as any hazard, condition, or activity that could reasonably be expected to cause injury, illness, or death to an employee. This includes hazardous substances, as well as any action, conduct, or comment (including of a sexual nature) that could cause physical or psychological harm to an employee.

    Note

    It is important to understand this due to the right of an employee to refuse dangerous work.

    Safety

    Safety means protection from danger and hazards arising out of, linked with or occurring in the course of employment. Safety here specifically refers to the protection of employees from hazards, conditions, or activities that could cause injury, illness, or death in the workplace. This includes ensuring that the workplace, equipment, and tools meet prescribed health, safety, and ergonomic standards, and that employees are adequately trained to use them safely.

    hazardous substance

    hazardous substance includes a hazardous product and a chemical, biological or physical agent that, by reason of a property that the agent possesses, is hazardous to the safety or health of a person exposed to it

    The Canada Labour Code defines hazardous substances as any chemical, biological, or physical agent that can be harmful to a person's health or safety. Employers are required to control the amount of hazardous substances in the workplace, store and handle them safely, and label them properly. This is to ensure that employees are not exposed to harmful substances that could cause injury or illness.

    Workplace

    A work place is defined as any place where an employee is engaged in work for their employer. This includes any location, whether indoors or outdoors, where work is performed, such as a factory, office, construction site, or vehicle.

    Employer

    An employer is defined as a person who employs one or more employees, or anyone acting on their behalf, as well as any employer organization. This includes individuals, corporations, partnerships, and other entities that hire employees to perform work.

    Employee

    An employee is defined as a person who works for an employer, including dependent contractors and private police officers. However, the definition excludes individuals who hold a managerial or confidential position that involves access to confidential information related to labor relations.

    Workplace Committee

    Work place committee means a work place health and safety committee established under section 135. A work place committee is a group of employees and employer representatives who work together to identify and resolve health and safety issues in the workplace. The committee is responsible for promoting and maintaining a safe and healthy work environment, and for making recommendations to the employer on how to improve health and safety conditions.

    Health and safety representative

    Means a person who is appointed as a health and safety representative under section 136. The health and safety representative is responsible for identifying and reporting workplace hazards, making recommendations to the employer for improving health and safety conditions, and participating in the investigation of workplace accidents and incidents. The health and safety representative also has the authority to accompany health and safety officers during workplace inspections and to participate in the development of health and safety policies and programs.

    Head

    Head means the Head of Compliance and Enforcement designated under subsection 122.21(1);

    Minister

    Minister means the Minister of Labour, this is an appointed position by the current govt.

    Harassment

    Harassment and violence means any action, conduct orcomment, including of a sexual nature, that can reasonably be expected to cause offence, humiliation or other physical or psychological injury or illness to an employee, including any prescribed action, conduct or comment;

    The Canada Labour Code defines harassment as "any improper conduct by an individual, that is directed at and offensive to another individual or individuals in the workplace, including at any event or any location related to work, and that the individual knew or ought reasonably to have known would cause offence or harm." This includes sexual harassment, which is defined as "any conduct, comment, gesture or contact of a sexual nature that is likely to cause offence or humiliation to any employee; or that might, on reasonable grounds, be perceived by that employee as placing a condition of a sexual nature on employment or on any opportunity for training or promotion."
