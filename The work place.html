    The work place

    According to the Canada Labor Code, the term "workplace" refers to any place where an employee is engaged in work for their employer.

    This can include a physical location, such as an office or factory, as well as other places where work is performed, such as a vehicle or a client's home. The definition of workplace is broad and is intended to cover any situation where an employee is carrying out work-related activities.

    The work environment

    Harassment and violence

    employers should take all reasonable steps to prevent and protect their employees from harassment and violence in the workplace.

    This includes developing and implementing policies and procedures to prevent and respond to incidents of harassment and violence, as well as providing employees with appropriate training and instruction on how to recognize and respond to such incidents.

    Employers are also required to investigate all incidents of harassment and violence in the workplace, and take appropriate corrective action to prevent further incidents from occurring.

    CLC part 2 section 125(1)

    (c) except as provided for in the regulations, investi- gate, record and report, in accordance with the regula- tions, all accidents, occurrences of harassment and vi- olence, occupational illnesses and other hazardous occurrences known to the employer;

    Harassment and violence is

    Any improper conduct by an individual, that is directed at and offensive to another individual in the workplace, including at any event or any location related to work, and that the individual knew or ought reasonably to have known would cause offence or harm. Harassment can take many forms, including verbal abuse, threats, intimidation, bullying, and unwanted physical contact. It can also be based on a variety of grounds, such as race, gender, sexual orientation, age, religion, or disability.

    First Aid

    Having trained personnel and proper equipment readily available can help prevent injuries from becoming more serious.

    Employees must have a first aid kit in their truck that is regularly inspected and kept complete. There will also be an eye wash station and first aid kit in the local office.

    Any injuries need to be reported to their direct supervisor.

    CLC part 2 section 125(1)

    (h) provide prescribed first-aid facilities and health services;

    Sanitary facilities

    Sanitary facilities in the workplace refer to the provision of clean and safe washrooms, toilets, and other facilities that promote good hygiene and sanitation.

    CLC part 2 section 125(1)

    (i) provide prescribed sanitary and personal facilities

    Water

    The employer must provide potable water, this is water that meets standards to be drinkable.

    All water must meet those standards in the workplace.

    CLC part 2 section 125(1)

    (j) provide, in accordance with prescribed standards, potable water;

    Work place surroundings

    To ensure the comfort of the staff make sure the surroundings meet prescribed standards.

    CLC part 2 section 125(1)

    (n) ensure that the levels of ventilation, lighting, tem- perature, humidity, sound and vibration are in accor- dance with prescribed standards;

    Safe entry and exit from the workplace

    CLC part 2 section 125(1)

    (p) ensure, in the prescribed manner, that employees have safe entry to, exit from and occupancy of the workplace;

    Ergonomics

    All activities in the workplace should follow ergonomic policy to prevent long term injury and disability.

    CLC part 2 section 125(1)

    (u) ensure that the workplace, work spaces and procedures meet prescribed ergonomic standards;

    Loose clothing

    Canada Occupational Health and Safety Regulations

    12.16 If there is a risk of injury in a work place that is due to loose clothing, long hair, jewellery or similar items, the employer must ensure that, if those items are worn, they are tied, covered or otherwise secured in or- der to eliminate or reduce the risk of injury

    Safety codes and standards

    Existing safety codes and standards relating to the activities of the employees should be implemented.

    CLC part 2 section 125(1)

    (v) adopt and implement prescribed safety codes and safety standards;

    The work vehicles

    This includes ensuring that the vehicles and equipment are safe to operate, properly maintained, and equipped with appropriate safety features. Employees should report issues with the vehicle to their direct supervisor for resolution.

    Employers are also responsible for providing their employees with appropriate training and instruction on the safe use of these vehicles and mobile equipment.

    CLC part 2 section 125(1)

    (k) ensure that the vehicles and mobile equipment used by the employees in the course of their employ- ment meet prescribed standards;

    Machinery, equipment and tools

    Make sure the tools, machinery and tools are regularly inspected and meet standards.

    CLC part 2 section 125(1)

    (t) ensure that the machinery, equipment and tools used by the employees in the course of their employ- ment meet prescribed health, safety and ergonomic standards and are safe under all conditions of their in- tended use;

    Defective tools and equipment

    In sections 13.9 and 13.10 of the Regulations, it is emphasized that employees are required to promptly report any defects in tools or machinery that could pose a safety risk. Furthermore, employers are obligated to label such items as unsafe and withdraw them from service to ensure the safety of their employees.

    Canada Occupational Health and Safety Regulations

    13.9 If an employee finds any defect in a tool or machine that may render it unsafe for use, they shall report the defect to their employer as soon as feasible.

    13.10 An employer shall mark or tag as unsafe and remove from service any tool or machine used by his employees that has a defect that may render it unsafe for use.

    Structural requirements

    Buildings and temporary structures

    This includes ensuring that all buildings and structures are designed, constructed, and maintained in a manner that is safe for employees to work in.

    Temporary structures may include things like scaffolding, tents, or other structures that are erected for a specific purpose and then dismantled.

    CLC part 2 section 125(1)

    (a) ensure that all permanent and temporary build- ings and structures meet the prescribed standards;

    Safely restricting movement around hazards

    The purpose of these safety measures is to prevent employees from falling from heights or into dangerous machinery or equipment.

    The prescribed standards for these safety measures may vary depending on the type of workplace and the specific hazards present.

    CLC part 2 section 125(1)

    (b) install guards, guard-rails, barricades and fences in accordance with prescribed standards;
